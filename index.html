We analyze 118 optical IFU data cubes of galaxies in voids from the CAVITY survey, to obtain their stellar population properties. We provide the global values, as well as the 2D maps of said properties.

We used the non-parametric full spectral fitting code STARLIGHT, in a wavelength range of 3750 to 7500 Å. The SSPs used were a combination of the ones of Vazdekis et al. (2010), from the MILES stellar library (for ages ≥ 63 Myr) and the sunthetic models from Gonzalez Delgado et al. (2005) for ages < 63 Myr. Both collections of SSPs assume a Salpeter IMF.

To obtain the spatially-resolved stellar population properties, we used the PyCASSO pipeline. Prior to the fit, we performed a Voronoi segmentation with a target SNR of 20. We offer a visualization toolComing soon! for the stellar properties of each CAVITY galaxy in our sample, which together with the results, will be public once CAVITY DR1 is released.

For more details about the methodology, check the article.

Global properties

The results table of global properties are offered in this FITS fileComing soon!. For each row of the BinTableHDU, we provide the following attributes:

Column Description
name Name of the CAVITY galaxy
HType Morphological classification as Hubble type: E, S0, Sa, Sb, Sc or Sd
TType Morphological classification as T-Type, from -3 to 10. Obtained from Dominguez Sanchez et al. (2018)
mass Total present stellar mass, in units of solar masses
log_age Logarithm of the light-weighted age, in units of years
SFR Intensity of the star formation rate, in units of solar masses per yr
sSFR Specific star formation rate, in units of Gyr-1
HLR Half-light radius, in kiloparsec

2D maps

We provide the 2D maps of the stellar population properties for each CAVITY galaxy in a single multi-extension FITS fileComing soon!. Each HDU has the maps of a different galaxy, whose name appears in the extension name. The attributes of the binary table for a given galaxy are:

Column Description
M_dens Stellar mass density, in units of solar masses per parsec squared
log_age Logarithm of the light-weighted age, in units of years
SFR_dens Star formation rate, in units of solar masses per Gyr and parsec squared
sSFR Specific star formation rate, in units of Gyr-1
